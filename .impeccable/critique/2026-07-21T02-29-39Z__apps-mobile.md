## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Not AI slop in copy or IA. The voice is distinctive and domain-fluent ("Your memory. Your keys. Now in your pocket."; the plaintext-leaves-the-phone confirmation naming the exact memory count). The failure axis is platform idiom: native-slop-adjacent. A fluent iPhone user recognizes it as not-quite-native within seconds, chiefly from Ionicons (not SF Symbols) and non-scaling type.

**Deterministic scan (code-evidence, native):** Palette is strong — cream on near-black is 14.92:1, the primary button (#ffffff on #2f6a54) is a healthy 6.34:1. Two real contrast FAILS: the selected memory-type chip `chipSelected` at 2.42:1 (ui.tsx:221) and the strike-through original in the audit view `maskOriginal` at 4.07:1 (converse-audit.tsx:179). Pervasive sub-44pt touch targets (memory-type chips ~33pt, all provider chips ~33pt, copy-links ~34pt, and the Converse "Providers" header button at ~20pt). Zero screen-reader live regions on any async/error/degrade state. `userInterfaceStyle:'automatic'` declared against a hard-coded dark-only UI. One unguarded infinite animation (ThinkingDots) with no Reduce-Motion check.

**Visual overlays:** none — no browser applies to a native app.

**Where A and B agree:** no Dynamic-Type-aware layout, sub-44 touch targets, Ionicons/custom tab bar, and sharing/providers density. **Where B caught what A missed:** Assessment A concluded contrast was fine and warned against fabricating a fail; the code scan found two genuine ones, including one in the trust-centerpiece audit view. **Correction the scan supplies:** A said the iOS text-size slider "does nothing"; in fact RN scales text by default, but the layouts are built for fixed sizes so chips and truncated fields clip when scaled — the real defect is fixed-size layout, not a dead slider.

## Overall Impression

This is a trustworthy-feeling product held back by an unfinished platform layer. The content, the honesty, and the system-status design are genuinely differentiated; the audit view ("What left this device") is a category-defining trust feature. What undercuts it is that the app reads as a competent cross-platform RN build rather than a native iOS app, and its signature value — "degrade loudly" — is literally silent to a VoiceOver user. The single biggest opportunity: make the trust the app already earns legible to everyone (Dynamic Type + screen-reader announcements) and give the sync paywall a dignified path, so the most motivated users don't end onboarding on a wall.

## What's Working

1. **"What left this device" (converse-audit.tsx) is category-defining.** Per-turn, verifiable proof of the exact outbound payload with a strike-through mask table. For regulated professionals this is the difference between marketing and proof — the feature most likely to win the audience.
2. **Sync-vs-Sharing is taught, not buried (sharing.tsx).** A loud warn banner states "The connector server can read what you share," then contrasts encrypted sync. Making the scarier truth loud is the rare, correct choice for this audience.
3. **System-status visibility is consistently excellent.** SyncPill never hides state, the Thinking animation prevents the 30s on-device load from reading as a freeze, the on-device banner affirms nothing is sent.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] No Dynamic-Type-aware layout; fixed sizes + truncation on identity fields.** Every fontSize is a hard-coded number (11–44). Text scales by RN default, but fixed-padding chips and numberOfLines={1} truncation on identity-bearing fields (provider label, audit endpoint, cardSub) clip when scaled. Excludes larger-text users and is the loudest non-native tell. Fix: a Dynamic-Type-aware token scale in ui.tsx, raise 11–13px muted floors, replace numberOfLines={1} with wrapping on identity fields. Command: /impeccable adapt.

**[P1] The "loud" privacy signal is invisible to VoiceOver.** Zero accessibilityLiveRegion / announceForAccessibility anywhere (Assessment B), so the invariant-#6 degrade banner, sync-failure state, and error notes never announce. Plus 3 of 4 Switches lack accessibilityLabel (create-vault, unlock, backup-secret). The app's differentiator fails its most vulnerable user. Fix: live regions on SyncPill/ErrorNote/WarningBanner/StatusBanner state changes; label the switches. Command: /impeccable harden.

**[P1] The sync paywall / private-beta path is a dead-end at peak intent (sync-setup.tsx).** Onboarding sells sync as the answer to "lose your phone, lose your vault," then "Turn on sync" lands on "Subscription required" (no price/link/path by App-Store policy) or "send your account id to support." The policy reason is invisible; by peak-end this wall may be the last thing a high-intent user remembers. Fix, within policy: explain why in-app purchase is unavailable, add one-tap "copy my account id" + "email support" (the id is already loaded), reaffirm local safety is unchanged. Command: /impeccable clarify.

**[P1] Tier-2 on-device name protection is unavailable on most devices, discovered late (providers.tsx, converse.tsx).** On-device pseudonymization needs Apple Intelligence / iOS 26; without it cloud chat is Tier-1 only and "rare or unusual names can slip through" — learned in the composer, after choosing cloud. For an EMS/therapy/legal audience, names are the sensitive payload. Fix: label each cloud provider with its effective tier on THIS device at selection time; make "What left this device" previewable before send, not only after. Command: /impeccable shape.

**[P2] sharing.tsx and providers.tsx overload the two highest-stakes screens.** Each does 5+ jobs on one scroll (sharing: connector config, share id, N scope toggles, per-scope confirm, pairing, app-write sync; providers: on-device card, saved list, presets, base URL, 40 discovery chips, key). Cognitive overload where deliberate decisions matter most. Fix: split into steps/sub-screens (Manage scopes / Pair an app / Sync; collapse the add-provider form behind one entry). Command: /impeccable distill.

**[P2] Quick technical wins.** Two contrast fails: chipSelected 2.42:1 (the Providers chip does the same pattern right at 7.61:1 — copy it) and maskOriginal 4.07:1 in the audit view. Sub-44pt touch targets, worst the Converse "Providers" header at ~20pt, plus adjacent edit/delete icons and chip rows. userInterfaceStyle:'automatic' should be 'dark' given the dark-only UI + fixed light StatusBar. ThinkingDots needs a Reduce-Motion guard. Commands: /impeccable colorize, /impeccable adapt.

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (first-timer):** onboarding offers four choices with no recommended default ("Link my Mac"/"vault file" mean nothing yet); providers.tsx is a developer console for a first chat; the sharing beta path (share id → "email support") is a wall Jordan can't cross alone.

**Casey (one-handed, distracted):** 13px chips and copy-links are small targets at the top of long scrolls; sharing.tsx needs two-handed scrolling through 8+ regions to share one scope; LockOnBackground re-auth on every glance-away is constant friction.

**Sam (accessibility-dependent):** the headline finding — no Dynamic-Type-aware layout means larger text clips; the loud degrade/sync banners are silent to VoiceOver (0 live regions); 3 unlabeled switches announce "switch, off" with no context. Contrast is otherwise fine (do not overstate) except the two fails above.

**Dana (EMS officer / therapist evaluating PHI trust — project persona):** top flag is Tier-2 unavailable on most phones today, so cloud chat is Tier-1 only and unusual patient/colleague names can slip — discovered mid-composer, not at provider choice. The audit view and the sharing confirm card are her strongest reasons to trust the app. Gap: no explicit data-handling/retention/BAA boundary statement for a compliance evaluator.

## Minor Observations

- create-vault enforces only an 8-char passphrase minimum for a vault with no recovery; no strength meter.
- memory/[id].tsx exposes entry_hash/prev_hash/raw metadata to end users — provenance is a strength for Dana, noise for Jordan; consider a details disclosure.
- Two greens (accent sage + #4cc38a "synced/copied") can read as two meanings.
- Ionicons + hand-rolled BottomNav forgo native large-title collapse, tab-bar material, and reselect-to-top; SF Symbols (expo-symbols) + Expo Router Tabs would erase the native tell cheaply.
- Safe-area handling exists only in BottomNav (bottom inset); headerShown:false screens use raw fixed padding.
- The danger button (#ffffff on #e5484d) is 3.91:1 — passes the 3:1 large-bold bar but under 4.5.

## Questions to Consider

1. If the strongest tier is unavailable on most phones shipping today, should cloud chat with un-pseudonymized names be offered by default to an EMS/therapy audience, or default to on-device-only with cloud as an explicit, warned opt-in?
2. The onboarding narrative sells sync as the fix for losing your vault, but the paywall is web-only by policy. Should sync even be surfaced in-app until there's a graceful path?
3. "What left this device" is your best trust asset, yet it only appears after sending. What changes if the user can preview the redacted payload before transmitting — proof-after becomes prevention-before?
4. You've chosen radical honesty (Tier-1 gaps, plaintext leaving, "your vault is gone if you lose the secret"). Where does relentless candor stop building trust and start manufacturing anxiety for a user who can't act on every caveat?
