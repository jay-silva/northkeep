## Anti-patterns verdict — does this look AI-generated?

**No — this is a disciplined, coherent tool, not slop.** One functional animation in the whole app; untrusted model/server text is rendered via el()/textContent (never innerHTML) as a security boundary; the privacy posture is enforced in control *state* (Tier "Off" disabled unless the endpoint is private), not just copy.

**Detector (code-evidence):** 3 warnings — a side-tab accent border (`.conflict border-left:3px`, line 108), a flat type hierarchy (sizes 11-22px clustered ~1.08x per step), and **30 em-dashes in body text** (a slop tell AND a violation of your own no-em-dashes-in-public-work rule). Palette is otherwise strong: body text 14.47:1, primary button 6.18 light / 5.73 dark (both pass).

**Where A and B agree:** the accessibility layer is the weak spot — missing focus styling, missing ARIA, and light-mode muted-text contrast failures. The design review flagged it as the #1 issue; the scan quantified it.

## Overall impression

A trustworthy-feeling, genuinely well-built desktop tool held back by an unfinished interaction/accessibility layer and an information architecture that needs banners to disambiguate itself. Status visibility and the show-don't-claim provenance design are exceptional; the gaps are keyboard/screen-reader support, a value-inverted confirmation vocabulary, and four overlapping "data leaves NorthKeep" concepts. Biggest opportunity: make the trust the app earns legible to everyone (contrast + ARIA + a non-tooltip privacy label) and scale confirmation rigor to actual exposure.

## What's working

1. **The per-turn provenance strip is the product's soul made visible** — badge + endpoint + model + actual tier applied + cost + disclosed memories, rendered textContent-safe so a hostile reply can't inject markup. Show, don't claim.
2. **Loud degradation everywhere (invariant #6)** — local-AI-off, semantic-search-unavailable, Tier-2-unavailable-names-not-pseudonymized all announced with the fix. A trust feature competitors skip.
3. **The share-confirm overlay + honest irreversibility copy** — the four-bullet "what leaves / where / what stays / undo" and "lose either and the vault is unrecoverable, by design." Stripe-grade consequence legibility at the scariest moment.

## Priority issues

**[P0] The accessibility floor is missing — on a compliance tool.** Light-mode muted secondary text fails WCAG AA everywhere (3.10-3.66:1; placeholder 2.68:1, failing even the 3:1 UI floor), and it colors nav labels, all `.prov` provenance lines, dialog descriptions, and disclosure copy. Zero `:focus-visible` rules (UA default only). Zero ARIA in 2687 lines: the 4 overlays have no `role="dialog"`/`aria-modal`/focus-trap and no Escape-to-close; the tabs aren't a tablist; and there's no `aria-live` on the errors, the degrade banner, or the PRIVATE/BOUNDED badge — the single most trust-load-bearing element is silent to a screen reader and its meaning lives only in a `title` tooltip. Fix: darken `--muted`/placeholder in light mode to >=4.5:1, add a global `:focus-visible` outline, add dialog semantics + focus trap + Escape, tablist roles, `aria-live` on status, a visible (non-tooltip) privacy label, and a reduced-motion guard on `.thinking`. → `/impeccable audit` then `/impeccable harden`

**[P1] Four overlapping "data leaves NorthKeep" concepts, disambiguated only by banners.** Connect (expose vault to a local MCP app) vs Sharing (copy scopes in *plaintext* to the connector server) vs Sync (encrypted, server can't read) vs "Sync now" (a third sync concept living inside Sharing). The product needs two paragraph-long banners to keep them apart — the necessity of that copy is the evidence the navigation is wrong, and the risk is a user sharing plaintext thinking it's encrypted sync. Fix: group the three external tabs under one "Connections" hub with a one-screen comparison; move/rename "Sync now." → `/impeccable shape`

**[P2] Confirmation vocabulary is inconsistent and value-inverted.** Custom overlays for share/setup/unlock/wizard, but native `confirm()`/`alert()` for forget/unshare/remove/Lock — and, most sharply, **granting an external AI "Everything (full access)" via Connect is a single click with no confirmation, while sharing one scope is double-gated.** The rigor is inverted relative to exposure. The Lock `confirm()` also overloads OK/Cancel as two affirmatives. Fix: route destructive actions through the `.overlay/.dialog` component; add a real confirm to the full-access Connect grant mirroring the share bullets; replace the dual-meaning Lock dialog. → `/impeccable clarify`

**[P3] Converse toolbar load + a Chat outbound-audit gap.** The highest-stakes surface asks the user to hold four interacting privacy controls (endpoint × model × tier × pin) before typing hello, and the privacy outcome is a 3-variable truth table whose meaning is tooltip-only. Separately, the exportable audit log covers what AI apps *read* from the vault (MCP calls) but not what Chat *sent out and at what tier* — the outbound record a QA/QI compliance buyer actually needs. Fix: collapse the toolbar to one "Privacy: PRIVATE/BOUNDED (Tier 1)" summary that expands; persist Converse provenance into the exportable log. → `/impeccable layout`

## Persona red flags

- **Jordan (first-timer):** setup wizard is strong, but a returning-empty user sees a bare "No memories match"; jargon wall on the Converse toolbar; a 5-way memory-type decision on "just remember this."
- **Alex (power user):** no keyboard tab nav, no command palette (Linear/Raycast bar this hard), no Escape to cancel a stream/overlay/editor.
- **Sam (accessibility) — weakest by far:** light-mode muted contrast fails AA; no `:focus-visible`; zero ARIA; the PRIVATE/BOUNDED badge and errors are silent to screen readers; overlays don't announce/trap focus; no reduced-motion guard.
- **Compliance evaluator:** loves the ciphertext-only copy, the audit log, CSV/JSON export, no-telemetry honesty. Will flag: the no-confirm full-access Connect grant; the absence of an exportable *outbound* Chat-redaction record; tooltip-only privacy semantics; and (ironically) contrast failures on the very copy that explains privacy.

## Minor observations

- 30 em-dashes in body copy violate your own public-writing rule; a mechanical fix and it removes a slop tell.
- Pervasive inline `style=` (120 instances) and hardcoded accent-tint hex (`#e6efe8`/`#22382c` etc.) instead of tokens — the light/dark pairs already drifted (a dark badge fails at 3.97 while its light twin passes at 5.40).
- Control-radius divergence (inputs 10px, the chat textarea 14px — same component, two radii).
- Cost hints and the env-override meaning are `title`-tooltip-only (keyboard/SR/touch-invisible).
- No responsive breakpoints; fixed 380/440/480/500px dialogs + `nowrap` badges risk horizontal overflow/clip in a narrow Tauri window.

## Questions worth sitting with

1. If the share-confirm overlay is your gold standard for consequential decisions, why does the *larger* exposure — granting an external AI full-vault MCP access — get zero confirmation?
2. The PRIVATE/BOUNDED badge is the most important pixel in the product, yet its promise lives in a tooltip and it's silent to screen readers. For a tool sold on trust, should the privacy state ever be hover-dependent?
3. You have an exportable record of what AI apps *read* from the vault, but not what Chat *sent out* and at what tier. For an EMS/legal/therapy buyer doing QA/QI, isn't the outbound record the one they need to defend?
4. If the copy has to work this hard (two paragraph banners) to disambiguate the navigation, is the navigation wrong?
